53rd Street:
A Walk Down Memory Lane

I moved to Hyde Park in 2023, right as I started grad school. This became my block, my coffee runs, my late-night CVS trips, the route I walked so many times I stopped seeing it.
But my friends started leaving, and I knew I'd have to leave eventually too. So before I did, I wanted to see what this place looked like before I got here; and to remember what it looked like while I was still here.

Let’s rewind.

Scroll down to jump to 2007, and walk west along 53rd.

First stop — 1520 E 53rd (Valois)

Valois has been at this location since 1960. By the time I arrived, it was already an institution. It has been the go-to spot for breakfast, gossip, and Saturday morning recovery for generations of students.

Let’s continue.

Keep walking west along 53rd to reach the old Dorchester corner.

Second stop — 1380 E 53rd (Dorchester Corner)

This corner has endured more than a few menu changes as we'll see. By the time I got here, this corner had already cycled through a few lives.

Now let’s turn and look to the left, toward what’s now the Sophy Hotel and Mesler.

Let’s keep going.

Turn back west along 53rd to see what surprises are waiting ahead.

Third stop — 1346 E 53rd
(Opposite Nichols Park)

This used to be a car wash and gas station, the kind of place you'd pass without thinking. In just a few years, this part of 53rd will feel completely different.

Almost there.

Back in 2007, we're heading a little further west to our final stop.

Final stop — 1301 E 53rd
(CVS & Kimbark Plaza)

One side of the corner barely changes at all, while the other flips through coffeeshops, grills, and restaurants.

Heading back.

We’ve reached the western end of our little tour in time. Let’s turn around and walk back east along 53rd, towards where we started.

Oh Wait!

We're still in 2025. Lit up, built up, and full of people. But before we finish, let’s rewind back to 2007 and continue the same path one more time.

Back where we began.

If you’ve lived in Hyde Park, you probably recognize at least one of these corners as “yours”: your go-to coffee spot, your first apartment, your default hangout spot.

We've only walked a kilometer or so, but we jumped almost twenty years. All of us - everyone who's ever lived here - showed up somewhere in the middle, overlapped for a few years, and built our own version of 53rd before moving on.

The street will keep changing. Valois will probably still be there. CVS will probably still come to the rescue at 3am. And wherever we end up in the world, this little strip of home will hold a version of us, frozen in time.
